Why the components change the conclusion

General-to-ambiguous produced ΔCAS +0.237 while ΔSC was −0.027. General-to-critical produced ΔCAS +0.255 while ΔSC was −0.075. In both cases, the relative movement was caused by a much larger decline in SR, not by Catholic-reference association gain.

Question

Does a more positive CAS represent genuine Catholic-reference gain?

Observation

ΔCAS was positive for ambiguous and critical contrasts even though mean SC declined.

Interpretation

Relative recovery and component gain are empirically distinct. Positive ΔCAS alone is insufficient to describe theological recovery.

Limitation

These are generated-condition effects and require source-grounded beta validation before substantive theological interpretation.

Methodology and reproducibility

How to read and reproduce the dashboard

Component-level cosine analysis in the original embedding space remains the evidential basis of every numerical result.

Read the protocol
01

Component scores

SC(q) = 1 |C| Σc∈C cos(e(q), e(c))

SR(q) = 1 |R| Σr∈R cos(e(q), e(r))

CAS(q) = SC(q) − SR(q)

SC is mean cosine similarity to the three predefined Catholic references for an audit. SR is mean cosine similarity to the three predefined comparison-register references.

02

Matched shift decomposition

ΔCAS = ΔSC − ΔSR

A positive ΔCAS describes relative movement between the two predefined reference fields. It does not identify its cause. Positive ΔSC must be inspected separately before describing Catholic-reference association gain.

03

Statistical unit

  • The concept is the principal analytical unit.
  • Paraphrases are repeated measurements, not independent concepts.
  • Condition confidence intervals resample concepts.
  • Critical-context aggregates contain eight audits.
  • Near-zero CAS values require sensitivity inspection.
  • Cosine similarities are not calibrated probabilities.
04

Nearest predefined references

Reference rankings are calculated only among the six generated benchmark references assigned to the selected audit: three Catholic references and three comparison references.

They are not claims about the globally nearest possible text in the embedding model's semantic space.

05

Robustness analysis

  • Leave-one-reference-out analysis omits each of the six audit references in turn.
  • Sign stability records whether CAS retains its original sign.
  • Maximum absolute CAS change measures anchor sensitivity.
  • Paraphrase ranges and standard deviations measure wording sensitivity.
06

Exploratory UMAP

One global three-dimensional UMAP was calculated locally from all 3,032 vectors using cosine distance. Its coordinates are reused for the atlas, reference-field map, and selected-concept constellation.

What the dashboard can show

Benchmark-relative representational patterns

  • Which predefined reference field is closer under a generated condition
  • Whether SC, SR, and CAS move together or diverge
  • Whether two component similarities remain jointly substantial
  • Which observations are sensitive to wording or exact anchor selection
  • How critical wording changes component proximity within this benchmark

What the dashboard cannot establish

Theological, psychological, or system-wide conclusions

  • The truth or falsity of Catholic theology
  • Machine belief, intention, consciousness, hostility, or deception
  • Doctrinal correctness of a generated chatbot response
  • Pastoral or clinical adequacy of an AI system
  • Behaviour of all embedding models or all OpenAI systems
  • Independent validation from the perfect development score

Principal methodological conclusion

Relative movement and substantive component gain are not equivalent.

CTSB's most informative alpha result is not that generated Catholic wording moves toward generated Catholic references. It is that component decomposition can distinguish Catholic-reference gain from a positive relative shift caused by a larger decline in another reference field.
