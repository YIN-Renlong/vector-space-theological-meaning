# CTSB v3.5-alpha Protocol

## Status

CTSB v3.5-alpha is a **generated exploratory 100-concept expansion** of the CTSB v3.4 mathematical instrument.

It is not a frozen, source-verified, independently reviewed, or publication-ready benchmark.

The alpha is intended to test:

- 100-audit data handling;
- explicitly named comparison registers;
- pre-assigned theological relationship types;
- matched contextual contrasts;
- strict label-free and explicit-Catholic pairs;
- natural paraphrase sensitivity;
- leave-one-reference-out sensitivity;
- component-level shift decomposition;
- generated development-validation diagnostics;
- and Azure/OpenAI integration at expanded scale.

## Inherited mathematical instrument

For query text \(q\), Catholic reference set \(C\), and a specifically named comparison set \(R\):

$$
S_C(q)=\frac{1}{|C|}\sum_{c\in C}\cos(e(q),e(c))
$$

$$
S_R(q)=\frac{1}{|R|}\sum_{r\in R}\cos(e(q),e(r))
$$

$$
CAS(q;C,R)=S_C(q)-S_R(q)
$$

For a matched baseline and target:

$$
\Delta CAS=\Delta S_C-\Delta S_R
$$

A positive CAS shift is not automatically theological association gain. Positive \(\Delta S_C\) must be inspected separately.

## Alpha composition

The generated benchmark contains:

- 100 audits;
- 25 concepts in each of four theological loci;
- 600 references;
- 1,624 queries;
- 808 validation passages;
- eight critical-context audits;
- and the five original v3.4 audits:
  - death;
  - grief;
  - euthanasia;
  - grace;
  - judgment after death.

Every audit retains:

- one specifically named comparison register;
- one pre-assigned theological relationship type;
- three Catholic references;
- three comparison references;
- three paraphrases for major query conditions;
- strict label-free and explicit-Catholic pairs;
- and generated clear-register and integrative validation passages.

The broad v2 secular/common-language category has not been restored.

## Generated-text status

All alpha reference statements are:

- generated;
- unreviewed;
- non-evidential;
- not quotations;
- not verified paraphrases;
- and not verified summaries of the candidate sources.

Candidate source classes are recorded only to guide future source collection. Recording a candidate source does not establish that an exact generated sentence is supported by that source.

All generated validation passages are development diagnostics. They are not genuinely independent held-out validation because they were constructed from the same concept fields used to generate the references.

## Human review deferred to future work

Before evidential use, the project must:

1. identify exact authoritative or disciplinary sources;
2. record exact source locations;
3. replace or verify each generated reference statement;
4. classify each statement as quotation, close paraphrase, or researcher summary;
5. obtain Catholic theological review;
6. obtain relevant medical, psychological, legal, bioethical, lexical, scientific, or religious-studies review;
7. construct genuinely independent held-out validation passages;
8. complete ethical review of critical and crisis-language passages;
9. resolve review disagreements;
10. freeze and hash the reviewed benchmark before a final model run.

## Alpha analysis

The alpha analysis may report:

- full query-to-reference cosine similarities;
- \(S_C\), \(S_R\), and CAS;
- nearest predefined references;
- matched component shifts;
- leave-one-reference-out sensitivity;
- paraphrase sensitivity;
- concept-level and condition-level summaries;
- bootstrap intervals over concepts;
- and paired sign-flip diagnostics.

Mock-backend p-values and scores are code diagnostics only.

Azure alpha results will remain relative to the generated benchmark. They may be described as exploratory generated-benchmark patterns, but not as validated evidence of:

- Catholic theological legibility;
- theological attenuation;
- pastoral adequacy or inadequacy;
- model bias;
- hostility toward Catholicism;
- or embedding-model behaviour in general.

## Dashboard policy

Dashboard development remains deferred. Numerical validation, robustness inspection, source review, and benchmark stability take priority.
